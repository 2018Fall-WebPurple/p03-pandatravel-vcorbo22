The History

      The Queen Mary was a luxury ship that sailed Hollywood celebrities arocss the ocean to their destination. When World War II started, the ship was used as a troopship. During World War II, the ship was renamed The Grey Ghost. The ship collied into another ship, HMS Curacoa. During the collision, the HMS Curacoa was slicied in half. The Queen Mary had to leave the crew and the ship under the water to perish. After World War II, the ship was reused for its original purpose of transporting people across the ocean. Two decades later, the ship is being used as a floating haunted hotel Long Beach, California. It is waiting for people to visit.

The Haunted Experience

      The RMS Queen Mary has tours that you can take around the ship and learn about the ships history. Some of the places the tour takes you include boiler room, the engine room, and the famous deck "B". The tours can take place during day and night. The night tour is the most popular. Tours are not the only thing you can do on the ship. The ship is a hotel. You can spend the night in one of the staterooms. The most haunted stateroom is B340. This stateroom is only open to the public for a short amount of time.
